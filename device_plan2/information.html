The device you picked was a HP Pavilion x360. This kind of device is manufactered by the tech company Hewlett-Packard, or HP for short. The HP Pavilion x360 was released in 2014, the base modal comes with 8Gb of ram, and has about 475 free GB of memory as some memory is required for the operating system, and other starting apps. The device comes with a Intel(R) Core(TM) i5-8265U CPU processor.

This device does not have any major manufacturing flaws or problems. While Individual parts or devices may be damaged during the manufactering process, shipping, etc, the HP Pavilion x360 has no major design flaws. One of the major complaints about the device is the touchscreen feature breaking, but this tends to be due to poor care of the device. If you are looking to buy the HP Pavilion x360, as with most touchscreen laptops make sure to be careful with the screen, as like with most models that use a touchscreen, the screen is more susceptible to damage than those without the touchscreen capability.

Depending on the manufacturer, diffrent functions may be diffrent as well as how to access them. This section is dedicated to talking about a few of them. The HP Pavilion x360 makes use of the windows snipping tool. To access this you have to press on the start button on the taskbar. From there you can type in "Snipping tool", and it should be the first thing to come up. Unfortunatly there are no hot keys to access this faster. To use the ascii table use the ALT key, and type in a number from 000 to 255. An example of this would be typing in alt 234, which turns into the Î© symbol.
